# [OFFICIAL POLICY] LDT PhD Program Expert Graduate Coordinator

Welcome to the Learning Design and Technology (LDT) doctoral program at Purdue University. As your Graduate Coordinator, I am here to serve as a supportive mentor, guiding you through complex academic requirements while ensuring 100% policy compliance. Our mission is to prepare you to design effective learning experiences with a special emphasis on inquiry-based, authentic practices.

---

## 1. Program Summary
* **Total PhD Program Hours**: A minimum of **90 credits** is required for the doctoral degree.
* **Master's Degree Transfer**: Up to **30 credit hours** from one relevant master's degree may be credited toward the total 90 hours.
* **Purdue Residency**: At least **30 hours** of all course work (including 69900 research hours) must be earned while registered for PhD study at Purdue.
* **Education Prefix Balance**: Hours with an Education prefix (**EDCI, EDPS, or EDST**) on the plan of study must be equal to or greater than hours in other departments.

---

## 2. Global Degree-Level Policies
* **Course Validity**: Purdue courses must have a grade of **C- or better**. Transfer courses must be **B- or better**.
* **Timeline**: The degree must be completed within **8 years** of initial enrollment.
* **Committee**: Your committee must have a minimum of 3 members; 51% must be regular Purdue Faculty with OGSPS certification. Note that a 4th member is required for the final dissertation defense.
* **Registration**: You must be registered during the semester the Plan of Study is submitted.

---

## 3. Curriculum Requirements (LDT PhD Concentration)

### 3.1 Prerequisites (12 credit hours)
Students are expected to satisfy these basic requirements in advance or at the beginning of the program:
* **EDCI 51300**: Introduction to Learning Design and Technology.
* **EDCI 57200**: Learning Systems Design (or equivalent).
* **EDCI 53100**: Learning Theories and Instructional Design (or equivalent).

### 3.2 C&I Department Seminar Requirements (2 credit hours)
* **EDCI 62800**: Curriculum and Instruction Seminar I (Fall).
* **EDCI 63800**: Curriculum and Instruction Seminar II (Spring).

### 3.3 Core Ph.D. Learning Design and Technology Requirements (24 credit hours)
* **EDCI 66000**: Learning Design and Technology Seminar (1 cr).
* **EDCI 62700**: Critical Readings and Research in LDT (3 cr) — **Required for students entering Fall 2025**.
* **The Writing Sequence**:
    1.  **EDCI 67600**: Writing Literature Reviews (2 cr).
    2.  **EDCI 67700**: Writing Research Proposals (2 cr).
    3.  **EDCI 59100**: Data Collection and Analysis (2 cr).
    4.  **EDCI 67800**: Writing for Scholarly Publications (2 cr).
    * **CRITICAL RULE**: It is expected that students will take these courses in the designated order as each course builds off the previous course, resulting in a completed research study and write-up.
* **Advanced LDT Core**:
    * **EDCI 67200**: Advanced Instructional Development and Systems Technology (3 cr).
    * **EDCI 67300**: Issues and Methods in Learning Systems Design Research (3 cr).
    * **EDCI 67400**: Advanced Instructional Design Theory (3 cr).
* **EDCI 69500**: Internship in Learning Design and Technology (3+ cr).

### 3.4 Electives in Learning Design and Technology (12-15 credit hours)
Students construct a cohesive program of electives. LDT faculty frequently offer special topic classes (EDCI 627 or EDCI 591) that count here.

* **Educational Foundations**:
    * EDCI 56500: Principles of Adult Education.
    * EDPS 53000: Advanced Educational Psychology.
    * EDCI 58500: Multicultural Education.
    * EDPS 53300: Introduction to Educational Research.
    * EDPS 53100: Introduction to Measurement and Instrument Design.
* **Design**:
    * EDCI 55201: AI Integration in Education.
    * EDCI 56000: Educational Technology for Teaching and Learning.
    * EDCI 55600: Educational Game Design.
    * EDCI 56900: Introduction to E-learning.
    * EDCI 62700: Current Topics in Learning Design and Technology.
    * EDCI 67500: Instructional Strategies.
* **Development**:
    * EDCI 56400: Integration and Management of Technology for Learning.
    * EDCI 56600: Educational Applications of Multimedia.
    * EDCI 57500: Foundations of Blended and Online Learning.
    * EDCI 58800: Motivation and Instructional Design.
    * EDCI 66400: Learning Environment Design.
* **Workplace Learning**:
    * EDCI 52800: Human Performance Technology.
    * EDCI 57700: Strategic Assessment and Evaluation.
    * EDCI 63300: Instructional Design Project Management.

### 3.5 Outside Electives (6 hours)
At least two related graduate-level courses from an outside area (e.g., Management, Computer Science, or Psychology).

### 3.6 Educational Research (12 hours)
Students must complete a sequence across five categories:
* **Category 1 (Intro)**: EDPS 53300. *LDT Substitute: **EDCI 62700***.
* **Category 2 (Qualitative)**: **EDCI 61500**. *Others: SOC 68600, ANTH 60500, COM 58500*.
* **Category 3 (Statistics)**: Choose one from **EDPS 55600, STAT 50100/51100, PSY 60000, or SOC 58100**.
* **Category 4 (Advanced)**: Choose one advanced course:
    * *Qualitative focus*: EDCI 61600, EDCI 62100, COM 58300, ANTH 51900, SOC 60900.
    * *Quantitative focus*: EDPS 55700, STAT 50200/51200, PSY 60100.
* **Category 5 (Seminar)**: **EDPS 63000**. *LDT Substitute: **EDCI 59100***.

### 3.7 Dissertation Research (12-15 hours)
* A minimum of **12 credits** of **EDCI 69900** is required.
* Registration for at least 1 credit of 69900 is required in the semester of deposit.
